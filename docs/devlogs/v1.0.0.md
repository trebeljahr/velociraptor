# Raptor Runner v1.0.0 — Launch

After a long stretch of tinkering in the margins between other projects, Raptor Runner is out.

It's a pixel-art take on the Chrome offline dinosaur — you jump over cacti, you try to survive, the score ticks up — but I wanted it to be *more* than a one-note endless runner. So:

## What you get at launch

- **A real day/night cycle.** Sky colour drifts through dawn, midday, golden hour, dusk, and full night as you keep running. The moon has actual phases that match a real 29.5-day lunar cycle.
- **A starry sky at night**, including a Milky Way band and the occasional shooting star once you've survived past the first few nights.
- **Weather.** Rain, thunderstorms with lightning, and the occasional snow flurry. The storm audio and ground feel change with the conditions.
- **Rare events.** Comets, meteor streaks, UFOs (with tractor-beam), tumbleweeds. And — if you're playing in December — a Santa fly-by.
- **Achievements.** Not a huge list yet, but the shape of a progression loop: first night survived, ten nights, twenty nights, first storm, first rainbow, seeing the full moon.
- **Local high scores** persist across sessions.
- **Gamepad support** — menu nav and in-game. Xbox, PlayStation, and Switch Pro all work.

## Platforms

- **Web / PWA** at `raptor.trebeljahr.com`. Installable, offline-capable, service-worker-cached.
- **Desktop builds** for macOS, Windows, and Linux, shipped via itch.io through the butler pipeline. Electron under the hood.

## What it's built on

Vanilla TypeScript + HTML5 Canvas. No framework, no game engine. Web Audio for SFX and music. Vite for the build. The rendered bundle is ~120 kB gzipped for the main JS. All hand-coded — which was kind of the point.

A slim release, but a real one. The core loop is the loop I wanted to ship.
