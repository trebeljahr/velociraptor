About Raptor Runner

A pixel-art homage to the Chrome "No Internet" dinosaur game, with a full day/night cycle and a starry sky.

The short version

I built the first version of this game in April 2022 on p5.js over about four days. It ran, barely — janky jump physics, one cactus sprite, no menu, no polish. I shelved it and moved on.

On April 10 & 11, 2026 — across roughly four hours of actual coding time — I brought it back to life using Claude Code as a pair programmer. What came out the other side is a different game: a full rewrite in vanilla canvas, a twelve-band day/night cycle with sun and moon on parabolic arcs, a rotating star field with a Milky Way band, per-frame accurate accessory anchoring, persistent unlocks, a shareable score card, and a lot of other small things.

What's new in the 2026 revival

  • Dropped p5.js, rewrote the whole thing in vanilla HTML canvas + requestAnimationFrame. No framework, no build step, no dependencies.
  • Eight new cactus sprites with hand-authored concave collision polygons, so near-misses actually feel like near-misses.
  • Fixed jump physics (the 2022 version was velocity-over-time nonsense) and added proper delta-time / frame-rate independence so the game runs at the same real-time speed on a 60Hz display, a 120Hz one, or a throttled browser.
  • Full twelve-band day/night cycle: blue day → magenta twilight → night → sunrise, with sun and moon tracing matching parabolic arcs and the sun color tinting from near-white at zenith through deep red at the horizon.
  • A rotating star field with ~300 twinkling stars and a tilted Milky Way band of denser stars + diffuse haze puffs. Stars appear the same every night regardless of score.
  • Sky-light tint on the raptor, cacti, ground, and clouds via an offscreen-canvas source-atop compositing pass, so dusk reads as dusk on the whole scene.
  • Start screen, settings menu, imprint / this page, music, SFX controls, landscape-guard for mobile portrait.
  • Persistent high score, persistent mute preference, and cosmetic unlocks at 100 jumps (party hat) and 200 jumps (thug-life glasses) — all saved to localStorage.
  • Shooting stars from the second night onward, drawn from a pre-baked ImageBitmap sprite so they don't hitch the first time they appear.
  • Shareable score card generated in a Web Worker using OffscreenCanvas, with a spinner shown immediately on game-over and the image swapped in when the worker returns. On desktop, copies image + text together to the clipboard in a single ClipboardItem. On mobile, hands it to navigator.share.
  • PWA manifest, proper Open Graph / Twitter Card metadata, and a refreshed OG preview composed from the in-game sprites.

Why a blog post, why a dinosaur game?

I have a pile of half-finished side projects that have been sitting in folders for years. AI pair programming has finally broken the "so close to done but never done" spell on them — the finishing work that used to feel like a slog (physics tuning, compositing passes, collision polygons, copy, meta tags) is now genuinely fun, because the grunt work has a collaborator.

Raptor Runner is the first one out the door. More are on the way.
